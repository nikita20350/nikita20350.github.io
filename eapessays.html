Community Supported Agriculture

        Although buying products directly from local farmers has some clear benefits, there are also many drawbacks, which stop many people from participating in this initiative.

        The first thing that comes to mind when we think about food from farms nearby is the quality and freshness. Eating organic products has numerous health benefits because of higher vitamin content and no chemicals used. Another plus of doing this is the impact we have on the environment: it promotes sustainable farming and minimizes the carbon footprint.

        Nevertheless it is clear why many people choose not to do it. Buying from farmers directly is usually more expensive and saving money is a priority for many. Also if a big portion of supermarkets’ customers decided to spend their money elsewhere, the quality of products in shops would go down along with the cost and the salary of the workers.

        Personally, I would support this initiative, because I prioritize my health when shopping for groceries. So around 70% of my total spendings on food a year might go directly to farmers.
